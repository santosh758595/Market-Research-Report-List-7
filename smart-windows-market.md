<p><p>The Smart Windows market comprises two key types: Active Glasses and Passive Glasses. Active Glasses utilize adjustable optics, allowing users to control light transmission and visibility dynamically, often through electronic means. These are commonly used in applications requiring real-time adaptability, like architectural and automotive designs. Passive Glasses, on the other hand, are simpler and do not require electronic components; they typically employ materials that automatically adjust to light conditions, offering lower costs and maintenance, suitable for more static environments.</p></p>
