<p><p>The general-purpose disk arrays market includes two main types of protocols: block and file protocols. Block protocols, such as iSCSI and Fibre Channel, provide direct access to storage blocks, ideal for databases and transactional workloads. File protocols, like NFS and SMB, offer a more user-friendly interface for file-sharing applications. NVMeOF protocols leverage NVMe technology over Fabrics, enabling high-speed data transfers and reduced latency, making them suitable for high-performance computing and big data applications. Together, these protocols cater to diverse storage needs across sectors.</p></p>
