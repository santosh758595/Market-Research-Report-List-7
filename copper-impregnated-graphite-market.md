<p><p>The copper impregnated graphite market features two primary types: pressed grade and molded grade graphite. Pressed grade graphite is formed under high pressure, resulting in a dense, uniform material suitable for high-load applications. Molded grade graphite, on the other hand, is created using a mold and typically exhibits better flexibility and shape retention. Both types enhance thermal and electrical conductivity, making them ideal for various industrial applications, including electrical contacts, bearings, and heat sinks in demanding environments.</p></p>
