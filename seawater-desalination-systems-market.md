<p><ul><li>ReverseOsmosis(RO)</li><li>MultistageFlash(MSF)</li><li>Multi-EffectDistillation(MED)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Seawater desalination systems primarily include three market types: Reverse Osmosis (RO), Multi-Stage Flash (MSF), and Multi-Effect Distillation (MED). RO employs a semi-permeable membrane to separate salt from water, making it energy-efficient and widely used. MSF utilizes heat to evaporate seawater and condense it into freshwater, offering high capacity but requiring significant energy. MED operates similarly to MSF but uses multiple evaporation stages, enhancing efficiency. Each system caters to different operational needs and resource availability, influencing selection in various applications.</p></p>
