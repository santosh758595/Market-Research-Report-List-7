<p><p>The thermal imaging cores market is primarily categorized into two types: uncooled thermal cores and cooled thermal cores. Uncooled thermal cores operate at ambient temperatures and use materials like microbolometers, making them smaller, lighter, and more cost-effective, ideal for portable applications. In contrast, cooled thermal cores utilize cryogenic cooling techniques to enhance sensitivity and image quality, suitable for high-performance applications such as military and aerospace. Each type serves distinct market needs based on performance, cost, and application requirements.</p></p>
