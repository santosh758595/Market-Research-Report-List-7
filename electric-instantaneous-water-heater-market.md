<p><p>The electric instantaneous water heater market is divided into two main types: whole-house and point-of-use systems. Whole-house units provide hot water for multiple outlets simultaneously, ensuring a consistent supply for appliances and fixtures throughout the home. In contrast, point-of-use heaters are designed for specific fixtures, such as sinks or showers, offering immediate hot water at the source. This targeted approach reduces wait times and energy waste, making each type suitable for different consumer needs and household configurations.</p></p>
