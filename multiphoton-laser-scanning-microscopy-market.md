<p><p>Multiphoton Laser Scanning Microscopy (MPLSM) encompasses two primary market types: upright microscopes and inverted microscopes. Upright microscopes are designed for observing samples placed on top of the stage, making them ideal for imaging thick specimens and tissues. In contrast, inverted microscopes have the optical components positioned below the stage, allowing for easier access to cell cultures and living organisms, particularly in biological and medical research. Both types leverage multi-photon techniques to achieve high-resolution, deep tissue imaging.</p></p>
