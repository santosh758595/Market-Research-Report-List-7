<p><p>Disability Income Protection Insurance consists of two main market types: private disability insurance and government disability insurance. Private disability insurance is offered by commercial insurers, providing coverage that replaces a portion of income lost due to disability, usually through employer-sponsored plans or individual policies. In contrast, government disability insurance is typically funded by taxpayers, offering benefits through programs like Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI), which support individuals unable to work due to disabilities for a specified duration.</p></p>
