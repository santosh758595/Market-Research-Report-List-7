<p><p>The hydrolyzed milk protein market consists of two primary types: whey protein and casein protein. Whey protein is derived from the liquid portion of milk that separates during cheese production, known for its rapid digestion and high bioavailability, making it popular among athletes and fitness enthusiasts. Casein, on the other hand, is the solid component that coagulates during cheese-making, offering a slow-release protein, ideal for muscle recovery and satiety. Both variants cater to different nutritional needs and consumer preferences.</p></p>
