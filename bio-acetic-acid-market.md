<p><ul><li>Grain and Sugar Fermentation</li><li>Wood Cellulose Fermentation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bio-acetic acid market primarily comprises two fermentation types: grain and sugar fermentation and wood cellulose fermentation. Grain and sugar fermentation utilizes crops like corn and sugarcane as feedstocks, converting sugars into acetic acid through microbial processes. In contrast, wood cellulose fermentation focuses on breaking down lignocellulosic biomass, such as wood waste, to extract sugars that are then fermented to produce acetic acid. Together, these processes play a vital role in sustainably producing bio-acetic acid for various industrial applications.</p></p>
